• AI And Information
    System In Business

    Artificial intelligence, better known as AI, is defined as being the simulation of human intelligence in machines that have been programmed to interact as a human (Burns, n.d.). This is a system developed to perform tasks that require human intelligence.

    There are two types of AI:

    •Narrow AI: This type of AI is known as weak; the AI is able to perform specific predefined functions but lacks the intelligence of humans.
    •General AI: This type of AI is known to be strong; this AI system has the ability to understand, learn, as well as apply the knowledge it has across a wide range of tasks.


    AI applications span various industries, like healthcare, finance, education, manufacturing and many more. As technology continues to advance, so do the capabilities of AI, leading to new possibilities and challenges.


    The effects of AI impact various aspects of society, the economy, and individual lives. Effects like:


    • Automation of jobs:
    AI is able to automate routines and repetitive tasks, increasing efficiency. Unfortunately, it caused job displacement in certain industries.


    • Increasing efficiency and productivity:
    AI systems perform tasks faster as well as more accurately, this leads to an increase of efficiency and productivity in the various sectors.


    • Enhanced experience:
    AI-powered assistants improve customer services by providing quick responses to inquiries.


    • Data analysis and insights:
    AI enables businesses to analyze very large amounts of data at a quicker rate. This improves competitiveness.


    • Cost savings:
    Through automation, improved efficiency businesses are able to save and cut costs.

    These effects of AI play a very significant role in businesses across various industries.


    What are information systems?

    Information system is a set of interconnected components that are able to work together to collect, process, store as well as spread information to support decision-making, coordination analysis, and visualization in an organization. Information systems play a very important role in the functioning of businesses as well as various organizations.


    Key components of information system include:

    •Hardware:
    the physical devices that are used to input, process, output and store data. These are devices like computers, servers, networks and more.

    •Software:
    these are the programs as well as the applications used to enable the hardware to perform specific functions.

    •Data:
    Raw facts and information are processed and transformed into meaningful information. Data is a crucial component of information system it is used to support decision-making.


    The effective management of information systems is crucial for organizations to remain competitive as well as making informed decisions in the business environment.


    Effects of information system on businesses


    Information systems have a significant effect, influencing various aspects of businesses. The effects of information systems on businesses include:


    •Increasing efficiency:
    Through information systems tasks are automated, reducing manual effort and improving the overall efficiency, allowing employees to work on more complex tasks.
    •Better communication and collaboration:
    Through information systems there is an increase in communication and collaboration within the business, increasing the efficiency as well as the production.
    •Enhance decision-making:
    Timely, accurate, and relevant information is provided to decision-makers. This enables them to make more accurate and informed decisions.
    •Data security and compliance:
    Information systems is able to assist in implementing security measures to protect data from unauthorized access as well as ensuring compliance with regulations and standards.
    •Cost saving:
    There are initial costs that are associated with the implementation as well as the implementation of the information system. The long-term benefits are significant in cost saving.

    The effects of information systems on businesses are positive (Limaye, 1998). Unfortunately, there are many challenges as well as risks that are fact.
